# Economics Projects

This track combines economic reasoning, data, and Python. Every project must distinguish description from causation and must explain what the indicators can and cannot prove.

## 01 — Inflation and Purchasing-Power Monitor

### Problem
Build an application that measures how inflation changes the purchasing power of money and the real value of income over time.

### Core requirements
- accept or import a CPI series;
- calculate monthly and annual inflation;
- build a cumulative price index;
- convert nominal income into real income;
- calculate the future cost of a basket under different inflation scenarios;
- compare official inflation with a custom household basket;
- handle missing observations;
- produce tables, charts, and a written interpretation.

### Research questions
- How is CPI constructed?
- What is the difference between inflation, the price level, and purchasing power?
- Why can an individual household experience inflation differently from the official index?

### Extensions
- Senegal household basket;
- food versus non-food inflation;
- wage growth versus inflation;
- scenario analysis for savings.

### Deliverables
Source code, documented data, charts, tests, and a short note explaining the economic meaning of the results.

## 02 — Growth and Development Dashboard

### Problem
Analyse the long-term development of Senegal or another African economy using a coherent set of macroeconomic indicators.

### Core requirements
- collect GDP, population, GDP per capita, inflation, unemployment, investment, and trade data;
- distinguish nominal from real variables;
- calculate growth rates and rolling averages;
- identify major periods and possible structural breaks;
- compare the selected country with at least two relevant peers;
- create a clean dashboard or report;
- explain data limitations and definition differences.

### Research questions
- Why can GDP grow while living standards improve slowly?
- What is the difference between extensive and intensive growth?
- Which indicators are useful for development beyond GDP?

### Extensions
- sector contribution to growth;
- productivity proxy;
- convergence analysis;
- commodity dependence;
- demographic scenarios.

### Deliverables
A reproducible data pipeline, visual analysis, country comparison, and a two-page economic brief.

## 03 — Sovereign Debt Sustainability Simulator

### Problem
Build a scenario model for the evolution of public debt relative to GDP.

### Core requirements
- model initial debt, interest rate, nominal GDP growth, inflation, primary balance, and exchange-rate effects;
- project debt-to-GDP over multiple years;
- create baseline, optimistic, and adverse scenarios;
- perform sensitivity analysis on growth, interest rates, and primary balance;
- identify conditions under which debt stabilises or rises;
- separate domestic and foreign-currency debt when data permit;
- explain assumptions clearly.

### Research questions
- What is the debt snowball effect?
- Why does the interest-growth differential matter?
- Why is debt sustainability not determined by one fixed threshold?

### Extensions
- stochastic shocks;
- debt-service-to-revenue ratio;
- maturity structure;
- fan charts;
- comparison with IMF or World Bank projections.

### Deliverables
A scenario engine, charts, sensitivity tables, tests, documented sources, and a concise policy memo.

## Completion standard
An economics project is complete when the data sources are traceable, nominal and real concepts are not confused, assumptions are explicit, and conclusions remain proportionate to the evidence.
